BMI & BFP Description

The Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measurement that evaluates the relationship between weight and height. It is widely used as a simple and quick method to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight. Based on the BMI value, individuals are generally categorized into different weight ranges, such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obese.

Body Fat Percentage (BFP) is the proportion of fat tissue in relation to total body weight. It provides a more detailed assessment of body composition and can be useful in evaluating overall health and fitness. The ideal body fat percentage varies depending on factors such as gender, age, and fitness level. Athletes often have lower body fat percentages, while higher percentages may indicate a need to focus on improving fitness and reducing fat.

BMI & BFP Ranges

BMI:

  • <18.5: Underweight
  • 18.5-24.9: Normal weight
  • 25-29.9: Overweight
  • 30+: Obesity

BFP (Men & Women):

  • <6%: Essential fat
  • 6-24%: Athletes
  • 25-31%: Fitness
  • 32%+: Acceptable
